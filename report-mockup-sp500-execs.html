Key Changes

Intel Corp. | Leadership Page Sep 16 CEO

Leadership page updated: Pat Gelsinger removed as CEO. Interim co-CEO structure announced — David Zinsner (CFO) and Michelle Johnston Holthaus (GM, Intel Products) serving as co-CEOs during board-led search. Frank Yeary named interim Executive Chair. Gelsinger "retired" amid board pressure over IDM 2.0 execution and $7B+ quarterly foundry losses.

Significance: marks the end of Intel's aggressive foundry pivot strategy. Stock rose 5% on departure — market interpreting as potential for strategic review including foundry spin-off or sale. Co-CEO structure historically unstable; permanent appointment expected within 90 days. Board evaluating whether to continue Intel Foundry Services or explore structural alternatives.

Starbucks Corp. | About Us Sep 9 CEO

Leadership page overhauled: Brian Niccol added as Chairman and CEO, effective September 9. Replaces Laxman Narasimhan who served just 16 months. Niccol recruited from Chipotle where he led the chain's digital transformation and 800% stock appreciation over six years. Compensation package includes $113M in total first-year awards plus a commuting arrangement from Newport Beach to Seattle via private jet.

Significance: Starbucks stock surged 24.5% on announcement day — largest single-day gain in company history and $21B in market cap creation. Niccol is expected to simplify the menu, reinvigorate in-store experience, and reset the China strategy. Chipotle stock dropped 7.5% the same day on his departure.

Nike Inc. | Leadership Page Sep 19 CEO

Leadership page updated: Elliott Hill named President and CEO, effective October 14. Hill is a 32-year Nike veteran who retired in 2020 as President of Consumer & Marketplace. Replaces John Donahoe whose tenure saw Nike lose market share to On Running and Hoka while over-rotating to direct-to-consumer at the expense of wholesale partnerships.

Significance: stock jumped 9.2% after-hours — market welcoming return of a "product person" after Donahoe's tech-executive approach. Hill's deep wholesale relationships expected to rebuild Foot Locker / Dick's partnerships. His first priority: restore innovation pipeline and reverse the "if you don't have Air Force 1s, you don't have Nike" criticism.

CVS Health Corp. | Newsroom Sep 22 CEO

Press release: David Joyner appointed President and CEO, effective immediately. Karen Lynch departed after Glenview Capital (4.5% stake) pushed for leadership change amid three consecutive quarters of lowered guidance. Joyner was President of Aetna, CVS's health insurance unit. Roger Farah named independent Board Chair.

Significance: activist-driven CEO change — Glenview had been pressuring for either a leadership change or breakup of the CVS-Aetna-Caremark conglomerate. Joyner's appointment from the insurance side suggests the board sees Aetna as the strategic priority over retail pharmacy. Watch for potential Caremark PBM spin-off evaluation.
