Key Changes

DOJ Antitrust Division | Newsroom Aug 5 Antitrust

New press release: "Court Rules Google Illegally Maintained Monopoly in Search." Judge Amit Mehta of the D.C. District Court found that Google's $26.3B in annual payments to Apple, Samsung, and Mozilla for default search placement constituted illegal monopoly maintenance under Section 2 of the Sherman Act. Google holds 89.2% of the U.S. general search market and 94.9% on mobile.

Significance: most important antitrust ruling in tech since U.S. v. Microsoft (2001). Remedies phase begins 2025 — DOJ is expected to seek structural remedies including potentially forcing Google to divest Chrome or Android, or banning exclusive default search agreements. Alphabet stock dropped 4.6% on the ruling. Apple's $20B+ annual Google TAC payment is at direct risk.

DOJ Criminal Division | Press Releases Jul 7 Criminal

New press release: "Boeing to Plead Guilty to Criminal Fraud Conspiracy Charge." Boeing agreed to plead guilty to conspiring to defraud the FAA during the 737 MAX certification process that led to two crashes killing 346 people (Lion Air 2018, Ethiopian Airlines 2019). Penalty: $243.6M fine (matching the 2021 DPA amount), an independent compliance monitor for 3 years, and $455M investment in compliance and safety programs.

Significance: first criminal guilty plea by a major U.S. aerospace company. Families of crash victims opposed the deal as too lenient. Independent monitor will have sweeping access to Boeing's safety and engineering processes. A felony conviction could jeopardize Boeing's eligibility for certain government contracts — though DOJ carved out an exception. Credit rating agencies reviewing implications.

CFPB | Newsroom Jul 11 Consumer Protection

New press release: "CFPB Orders Bank of America to Pay $250 Million for Systematic Junk Fees and Fake Accounts." CFPB found Bank of America (1) double-dipped on insufficient funds fees on the same transaction, (2) withheld promised credit card rewards from customers, and (3) opened accounts without customer authorization. Penalty: $150M to affected consumers + $90M CFPB penalty + $10M OCC penalty.

Significance: largest CFPB bank enforcement action of 2024. The fake accounts finding draws direct comparisons to Wells Fargo's 2016 scandal. Bank of America neither admitted nor denied the findings. CFPB Director Chopra called it "one bank, three different schemes to cheat customers." Expect Congressional scrutiny at upcoming Senate Banking Committee hearings.

DOJ Antitrust Division | Press Releases Aug 23 Antitrust

New press release: "Justice Department Sues RealPage for Algorithmic Pricing Scheme that Harms Renters." DOJ alleges RealPage's revenue management software enabled landlords to coordinate rent pricing using nonpublic competitor data — effectively an algorithmic price-fixing scheme. Named co-conspirators include several S&P 500 REITs: AvalonBay Communities, Equity Residential, and Camden Property Trust, collectively managing 500,000+ apartment units.

Significance: first major federal challenge to algorithmic collusion — could reshape how AI pricing tools are used across industries. Named REITs face both DOJ liability and a parallel class action seeking treble damages. If successful, this theory of algorithmic coordination could extend to airline pricing, hotel revenue management, and beyond.
